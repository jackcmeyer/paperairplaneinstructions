Paper Airplane Instructions

In this instruction guide, there are instructions for building a sleek, simple paper airplane. There are two forms of the instructions, a video tutorial and an instruction set with a step by step procedure for building and launching the paper airplane.

After successful completition of this guide, a paper airplane will have been built and launched.

This guide is inteneded for those of age 13 and older.


Start Building

Discover what all the buzz is about!

Check out the video below for a tutorial on how to build the plane.

Required Materials: One (1) 8.5in x 11in piece of paper.

Take a standard piece of paper and fold it lengthwise so that the edges are lined up. Unfold the piece of paper, and then refold it in the opposite direction to ensure there is a visible crease down the middle of the paper.

Unfold the piece of paper and place it flat on a hard surface. Make sure that the crease is visible.

Take the top two corners and fold them down so that the corners meet at the center crease of the paper.

Take the newly formed corners on the left and right edges and again fold them so that they meet at the center crease.

Take the left inclined side of the paper and fold it towards the center crease. Repeat this step with the right inclined side. For each side, there should be about an inch of paper going past the bottom of the paper if this step is done correctly.

Flip the piece of paper over so that the flat part of the paper is facing upwards.

Take your index finger and place it on the crease about 1/4 of the way down from the tip of the airplane.

Apply downward force to the crease so that the paper folds inward. Once you cannot push down any further, pinch both sides of the plane so that all the layers are pressed together.

Take the left most layer and fold it perpendicular to the other layers to form the first wing. Make sure that the remaining layers are still pinched together

Repeat this step with the right most layer so that it is parallel with the left most layer. After this step, you should still be pinching the five middle layers of paper.

You have created your very own paper airplane. Now it is time to make it fly.

Hold the airplane with your index finger and thumb about a quarter of the way down from the tip of the airplane

Pull the plane back so that it is parallel to your ear. Your elbow should form a ninety degree angle.

Extend your forearm forward and release the plane as you are doing so.
